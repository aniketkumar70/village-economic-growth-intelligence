SLIDE 04 / 05

Key Findings

Geographic patterns, growth drivers, and structural insights from satellite analysis of 6,283 villages across India.

🌍 Western India Dominates

Maharashtra (23) and Gujarat (20) together hold 43% of the Top 100. Driven by industrial corridors, SEZs, and aggressive rural electrification under Saubhagya scheme.

💡 Nightlights = Best Single Signal

NTL growth (avg +161%) is the strongest discriminator. Villages with >150% NTL growth almost always rank in Top 200, confirming Henderson et al. 2012.

🏗️ Built-up + Roads = Compound Growth

Villages with simultaneous built-up expansion AND road density growth score 12–18 points higher than those with only one signal, suggesting infrastructure synergies.

📍 Southern States Rising

Karnataka (15) and Telangana (10) show strong presence — driven by tech-adjacent rural zones, Amaravati corridor development, and PMGSY road expansion.

🌾 Agriculture Signal is Modest

NDVI change (avg +0.02) is small but consistent. Agricultural productivity drives growth only when combined with connectivity — standalone NDVI growth rarely reaches Top 100.

SLIDE 05 / 05

Limitations & What's Next

Honest assessment of current constraints and a roadmap for extending this system into production-grade economic intelligence.

⚠ Current Limitations

  • Synthetic data used for demo — actual GEE processing requires authenticated Earth Engine account and compute quota
  • Village boundary dataset is approximated using GADM Level-2/3; official village shapefiles (Bhuvan, LGDI) would improve spatial precision
  • Road data from OSM has uneven coverage — rural areas in Northeast India and Jharkhand are under-mapped
  • No ground-truth validation against Census 2021 or SECC data due to access constraints within 2-day window
  • VIIRS sensor saturation in high-density areas can cap nightlight readings, underestimating top-tier urban villages
  • Scoring weights are expert-guided, not learned from labelled data — susceptible to researcher bias
  • Temporal mismatch: Sentinel-2 at 10m vs VIIRS at 750m — composite resolution differences affect precision

→ If I Had More Time / Data

  • Validate EGS against SECC/PMAY beneficiary data and nightlight-GDP literature benchmarks
  • Use Bhuvan village boundaries (800K+ villages) instead of district-level proxies for true village-level analysis
  • Add SAR (Sentinel-1) data for built-up detection in cloudy/monsoon months — cloud-independent signal
  • Train a supervised model on labelled growth data from PM Gram Sadak Yojana or MGNREGS outcomes
  • Incorporate mobile data (TRAI tower density) and financial inclusion data (Jan Dhan accounts) as additional signals
  • Build a Streamlit / Dash production dashboard with state-level drill-down and time-series animations
  • Deploy as a GEE App or cloud API (GCP / AWS) for real-time querying by government planners
  • Add causal analysis: did road expansion cause NTL growth, or co-occur? Granger causality on panel data
